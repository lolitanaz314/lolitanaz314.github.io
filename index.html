Portfolio I’m an aspiring data scientist with a background in computer science, applied mathematics, and statistics from Stony Brook University. I previously worked as a software engineer at Google, where I contributed to both large-scale infrastructure and data science–focused products. Most recently, I worked on Google Meridian, Google’s open-source marketing mix modeling platform, implementing algorithms like Hill saturation curves, response curves, and adstock decay to help analyze marketing impact. My work sits at the intersection of modeling, experimentation, and product—turning complex statistical ideas into tools that help teams make better decisions. Outside of industry, I’ve spent years exploring machine learning through research and side projects—from predicting patient diagnoses with recurrent neural networks to using BERT models to analyze financial news and social media data. I also co-founded the Applied Math & Statistics Committee at Stony Brook, where I organized speaker events and built a community of 200+ students interested in quantitative fields. Today, I’m especially excited about applying data science to real-world problems in the creator economy, AI-powered tools, and measurement systems—areas where rigorous modeling and thoughtful product design can create real impact.
